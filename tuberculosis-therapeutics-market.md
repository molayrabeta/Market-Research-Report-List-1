<p><p>The tuberculosis therapeutics market encompasses various drug types aimed at treating TB infections. Isoniazid and Rifampin are first-line agents that target the bacterial cell wall and inhibit RNA synthesis respectively. Pyrazinamide enhances the efficacy of other drugs, while Ethambutol disrupts cell wall formation. Hydrazine derivatives serve as alternative treatments. Miscellaneous anti-tubercular drugs include newer agents that address drug-resistant strains. Collectively, these therapies are crucial for managing tuberculosis, improving patient outcomes, and combating disease spread.</p></p>
