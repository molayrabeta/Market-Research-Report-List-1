<p><p>The non-invasive neuromodulation device market includes several types, each providing unique stimulation methods to modulate neuronal activity. Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS) employs a steady current to alter cortical excitability. Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS) uses alternating currents for rhythmical neuronal modulation. Transcranial Pulsed Current Stimulation (tPCS) involves short pulses to enhance brain function, while Transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS) applies random noise to improve cognition. Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT) induces controlled seizures to treat severe mental health conditions. Other devices in this market further expand therapeutic applications.</p></p>
