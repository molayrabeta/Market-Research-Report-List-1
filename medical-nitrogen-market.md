<p><p>The medical nitrogen market is categorized into two main types: gaseous state and liquid state. Gaseous nitrogen is typically used in various medical applications, including respiratory treatments and anesthesia systems, due to its inert properties and ability to preserve anoxia. Liquid nitrogen, on the other hand, plays a crucial role in cryotherapy, cryopreservation, and laboratory processes, offering extremely low temperatures for biological samples. Both forms are vital in healthcare settings, supporting a range of therapeutic and diagnostic procedures.</p></p>
