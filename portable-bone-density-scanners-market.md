<p><p>The portable bone density scanners market includes two primary types: X-ray and ultrasound. X-ray bone density scanners utilize low-dose radiation to measure mineral content, providing precise assessments of bone health, commonly used in clinical settings. In contrast, ultrasound bone density scanners employ sound waves to estimate bone density without radiation, offering a safer and more convenient option for patients. Both technologies cater to different needs in diagnosing osteoporosis and assessing fracture risk, contributing to the growing demand for portable solutions.</p></p>
