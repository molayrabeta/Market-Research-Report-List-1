<p><p>The Oral Mucositis Therapeutics market is primarily segmented into two types based on treatment context: chemotherapy and radiotherapy. Chemotherapy-induced oral mucositis arises from cytotoxic agents damaging the mucosal lining, leading to painful sores and increased infection risk. Therapeutics focus on prevention and symptomatic relief. In contrast, radiotherapy-related mucositis results from targeted radiation exposure, often in head and neck cancer treatments, causing similar mucosal damage. Both segments require distinct therapeutic approaches, including mucosal protectants, anti-inflammatory agents, and pain management strategies.</p></p>
