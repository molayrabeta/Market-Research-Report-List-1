<p><ul><li>Chlorine Residual Sensor</li><li>TOC Sensor</li><li>Turbidity Sensor</li><li>Conductivity Sensor</li><li>PH Sensor</li><li>ORP Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The water quality sensor market includes various types designed to measure specific parameters. Chlorine residual sensors detect chlorine levels, crucial for water treatment safety. Total Organic Carbon (TOC) sensors measure organic compounds, indicating potential contamination. Turbidity sensors assess water clarity, vital for evaluating water quality. Conductivity sensors determine ion concentration, reflecting salinity and purity. pH sensors gauge acidity or alkalinity, essential for ecosystem balance. Oxidation-Reduction Potential (ORP) sensors evaluate the water's oxidative properties, important for chemical reactions in water treatment processes.</p></p>
